## 0. The one-line brief (Jobs' cut)

Most "AI automation" in Africa is either a consultant's slide deck or a Silicon-Valley tool that doesn't speak Naira, WhatsApp, or NDPR. Neoscona is neither. **Neoscona is AI workers that are already trained, already compliant, and already live on the channels Africans actually use — switch one on this afternoon, or have us build you a custom one.**

If we get this right, "Neoscona" becomes the verb. *"Just Neoscona it."*

## 1. Positioning — April Dunford

### 1.1 The positioning statement

> **For** African SMBs and enterprises drowning in repetitive customer work,
> **Neoscona is** an AI automation platform
> **that** gives you ready-to-run AI workers — receptionist, lead agent, follow-up — live on WhatsApp, voice, and Instagram in minutes,
> **unlike** dev agencies (too slow, too bespoke) or foreign SaaS (no local channels, payments, or data law),
> **because** every Neoscona worker is pre-trained, locally compliant, and grounded so it never makes things up.

### 1.2 The category we're creating

We do **not** compete in "chatbots." We name and own a new category:

> **Ready-to-run AI workers.**

A category nobody owns yet. Chatbots answer; *workers do the job* — qualify the lead, book the viewing, chase the follow-up, hand off to a human when it matters. Owning the category is how you become the monopoly: you're not the best option in a crowded list, you're the only one in a category of your own naming.

### 1.3 The Twilio parallel (the model we're copying on purpose)

| Twilio | Neoscona |
|---|---|
| Messaging API | **Neoscona Reach** (multi-channel: WhatsApp, IG, SMS) |
| Voice API | **Neoscona Voice** (AI receptionist, inbound + outbound) |
| Building blocks for developers | **Ready-to-run workers** for *operators* — no code required |
| Twilio Marketplace (add-ons) | **Neoscona Marketplace** (templates by us + partners) |
| "Communications, programmable" | "Automation, ready to run" |

Twilio gave developers primitives. **Neoscona gives every business a finished worker — and lets the ambitious build their own.** That's the wedge: no-code-ready templates first (reach the whole market), platform + marketplace second (own it).

### 1.4 The unique value (what only we can say)

1. **Grounded, not guessing.** Our anti-hallucination core cross-checks every price and fact against your real data. (This is real — it ships in Reva today. It is the single most defensible claim we have. Lead with it.)
2. **Lives where Africa lives.** WhatsApp-first, with Instagram, and voice. Naira pricing, Paystack billing, NDPR-aware.
3. **From template to bespoke, one brand.** Start self-serve in minutes; graduate to a custom system without changing vendors.
4. **Human-handoff built in.** The AI pauses the moment a person takes over. Trust, not replacement.

### 1.5 What we are NOT (discipline keeps the brand sharp)

- Not an agency that sells hours. We sell *outcomes that are already built.*
- Not a generic chatbot you have to train from zero.
- Not a foreign tool bolted onto African business. Built here, for here.

## 2. Naming architecture — the room, ratified by Jobs

**Master brand:** **Neoscona.** Everything is "Neoscona ___."

| Product line | Name | What it is | Status today |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI receptionist | **Neoscona Voice** | Answers every call, books, qualifies, 24/7 | Plumbing live (Vapi/ElevenLabs) |
| Lead engine | **Neoscona Reva** | Real-estate lead qualify + nurture + follow-up | Production-track (Reva) |
| Messaging layer | **Neoscona Reach** | One inbox across WhatsApp/IG | Live (gateway) |
| Custom workflows | **Neoscona Flow** | Bespoke automations for enterprise | Sell-as-service now |
| Ecosystem | **Neoscona Marketplace** | Templates by Neoscona + partners | Vision / phase 2 |

> **Naming rule (Jobs):** the product name says the *job*, never the tech. "Voice," not "Vapi-powered conversational IVR." A customer should understand what it does before they understand how it works. Keep "Reva" — it already has equity and a face ("Amara"); it becomes *Neoscona Reva*, the first worker in the catalog.

**Note for the build:** "Neoscona Reva" is the public, branded name; "Reva" stays the internal/product codename. No need to rename code — rename the *story*.

## 4. Visual identity direction — Paula Scher

> *Identity is a system, not a logo. Build a world a customer can recognize from across the street.*

### 4.1 Logotype

- **Wordmark-first.** "Neoscona" set in a confident, geometric-humanist sans (e.g. a refined grotesque — think the warmth of Söhne / the structure of Neue Haas). The word *is* the logo. No spider mascot, no AI-cliché glyph.
- Product lock-ups: `Neoscona Voice`, `Neoscona Reva` — master name in full weight, product name in a lighter weight. Twilio-style consistency.

### 4.2 The "Neo" mark

- A single, reducible monogram derived from the **N** — usable as app icon, favicon, marketplace tile. Geometric, one continuous form. Works in one color at 16px.

### 4.3 Color

- **Anchor:** deep, near-black **ink** (`#0A0A0F`) — authority, "enterprise-grade," the canvas.
- **Signature:** one ownable accent. The current site leans purple — **keep and sharpen a single confident violet** (`#6D45FF`-ish) as *the* Neoscona color. One accent, used decisively, is how brands become recognizable (Twilio red, Stripe purple). Don't dilute it across a rainbow.
- **Supporting:** warm off-white (`#FAFAF7`) for light surfaces; a restrained success-green only for "live/working" states.
- **Rule:** ink + white + one violet. If a third color is tempting, the answer is no.

### 4.4 Type & layout system

- Display: geometric-humanist sans, tight headlines, generous size jumps.
- Body: same family, comfortable line-height, never below 16px.
- **Grid & whitespace are the design.** Lots of air. Big type. Few words. (Jobs: "Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication.")
- Motion: subtle, purposeful — a worker "coming alive" (a status dot pulsing live), never decorative animation.

### 4.5 Iconography & illustration

- Custom, single-weight line icons for each worker (a clean phone for Voice, a chat node for Reva, a flow-graph for Flow). Consistent corner radius, consistent stroke. (The repo already has `wir-1..5` SVGs — audit and unify these into the new system rather than starting from zero.)
- Avoid stock "robot/AI brain" imagery entirely. We show *the work getting done* (a real conversation, a booked viewing), not sci-fi.

### 4.6 The feeling

Stripe's precision × Twilio's developer-trust × Apple's restraint — **localized**. Premium enough that an enterprise trusts it with their data; simple enough that an SMB owner sets it up alone.

## 5. Go-to-market & launch narrative — Seth Godin

### 5.1 The remarkable idea worth spreading

> "I let an AI answer my customers and it *never lies* and *never sleeps* — and I set it up in an afternoon."

That's the story a customer tells another customer. Our entire GTM is engineering that one sentence into existence and getting it repeated.

### 5.2 The minimum viable market (start narrow to go wide)

- **Beachhead: Nigerian real estate** (Reva already fits, anti-hallucination matters most where prices/inventory are concrete). Win this tribe completely.
- **Then adjacent high-missed-call verticals:** clinics, salons/spas, auto dealers, schools, logistics — anyone who lives on WhatsApp and bleeds money on slow replies. Neoscona Voice generalizes instantly.
- **Then horizontal:** Marketplace lets every vertical self-serve a template; partners build the long tail.

### 5.3 The ladder (self-serve → platform → monopoly)

1. **Templates win the market.** Free trial → live worker → "pays for itself with one saved deal." Frictionless self-serve is the growth engine.
2. **Platform deepens the moat.** Per-tenant config, inbox, billing — switching cost rises.
3. **Marketplace makes it a monopoly.** Once partners build on Neoscona and customers shop templates, network effects do the defending. *This is the endgame — every line above is laying track toward it.*

### 5.4 Launch sequence

- **Phase 1 — Repositioning launch.** New neoscona.xyz goes live: from "Web2/Web3 studio" to "AI automation platform." Anchor post on the blog: *"Why we're betting Africa runs on AI workers."* (founder manifesto — see §6).
- **Phase 2 — Worker spotlights.** One launch per worker (Voice, Reva, Reach), each with a real customer outcome. Build in public.
- **Phase 3 — Marketplace teaser + waitlist** for partners/builders. Scarcity + tribe.
- **Drumbeat:** the blog becomes a *category-defining* publication — playbooks on "AI workers for African business." Own the search term before anyone knows it's a term.

### 5.5 Distribution truths

- WhatsApp is the channel *and* the demo: let prospects message a live Neoscona worker and feel it.
- Founder-led, in public. The first 100 customers come from trust, not ads.
- Every customer is a case study. Outcome numbers (response time, leads saved) are the only ad copy that works.

## 6. The hero campaign & brand story — Lee Clow

### 6.1 The campaign idea

> ## "Meet your new team."

The campaign humanizes the workers. Each Neoscona worker is introduced like a star hire — a name, a job, a personality — except they never sleep, never forget, and never lie.

- **Film/hero visual:** an African SMB owner closing their laptop and going home — while, in soft glowing UI, the Neoscona workers keep working through the night: a call answered, a lead qualified at 2am, a follow-up sent at dawn. Tagline lands on black: **"Your business never sleeps. Now your team doesn't either."**
- This is the "Think Different" move: we're not selling software, we're selling *the feeling of finally having help you can trust.*

### 6.2 The manifesto (founder's anchor post / about page)

> **Most businesses in Africa don't lose to bad products. They lose to a slow reply.**
>
> A customer messages at midnight. Calls during lunch. Asks the same question a hundred others asked. And somewhere a deal quietly dies — not because the business wasn't good enough, but because no human could be everywhere at once.
>
> We built Neoscona for that gap.
>
> Not another chatbot. Not another consultant's deck. **AI workers** — already trained, already on WhatsApp, already honest about what your business can and can't do. Switch one on this afternoon. Or tell us what you need, and we'll build it.
>
> We believe the next decade of African business will be run by people *and* the tireless workers they hire from a screen. We're building the place you hire them.
>
> **Neoscona. AI automation, ready to run.**

### 6.3 The repeatable signature

- Sign-off on everything: **"Neoscona. Ready to run."**
- Visual signature: the live status dot (●) pulsing next to a worker's name — the universal "I'm working right now" cue. It becomes ours.
